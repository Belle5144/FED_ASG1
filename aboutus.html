This rabbit character has been created by a Dutchman, Dick Bruna. Obviously its original name is the Dutch nijntje, not the English miffy. As the Dutch word for rabbit is konijn, the character’s name is a (childish) abbreviation, simply leaving out the first syllable. And -tje is a diminutive ending, so: konijn without first syllable gives nijn with diminutive ending gives nijntje. Dick Bruna consequently had been avoiding capitalizing. Therefore it’s nijntje rather than Nijntje.
Together with his translator, the writer invented a new word/name that could equivalently be used in international editions of Nijntje. One agreed on “Miffy”. “Miffy” would be a name that could easily be associated with a (little) rabbit.
